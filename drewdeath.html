Artist Statement

Statement

Photography as the
practice of presence.

My work begins with the question of what it means to actually see something — not to observe, to record, or to aestheticize, but to be present enough that the moment leaves a mark before the mind names it.

Working primarily in Scotland in documentary and street traditions, I'm drawn to the ordinary thresholds of daily life: the light between actions, faces in transit, landscapes that ask nothing of you. The Leica M system is not a stylistic choice so much as a commitment to slowness — to prefocus, zone, and wait rather than hunt with burst fire.

Adequate Light — the project that runs alongside this practice — explores the same questions in writing: through mindufulness, philosophy of perception, and the ethics of witness developed by thinkers like John Berger. The photographs and the words are the same inquiry by different means.
